Pantry and Laundry Room

My first ever major project to organize my home was the pantry/laundry room. It took me roughly a month to a month and a half. It wasn’t easy for me because something always goes wrong. From painting the room to ordering too much of a product. All my ideas and inspirations were from Pinterest, The Container Store, and just me.

My first thing on the list was to paint the room. I just wanted a neutral/nude color. I wanted the color to match my farmhouse decorations from the kitchen. For the shelves, I wanted to put like a wallpaper design on to give it an appealing look, but it wasn’t sticking so in the end just painted the shelves. The shelves are painted a little darker from the original color of the walls. Honestly it looks much better that way.

Now into details with what type of storage bins/baskets for the pantry. I’m a person that doesn’t like to spend too much money. Through my inspirations, it showed me that you would have to spend a pretty penny if you wanted to match exactly what they had. It’s better to get ideas and make it your own. You don’t have to spend a fortune to have an organized pantry.

I decided to use bins which I bought from Big Lots. In my picture above you can see grey and white bins. I used those bins for stuff that were in boxes, snacks, and oil bottles. Each bin is labeled so everyone can know what’s inside of them. The baskets are from two places which is Big Lots and The Container Store. I used those baskets for items that were in bags and packages. The cans and jars were put on wire stands so it can act like two shelves rather than just one shelf. Its nice to see what you have and what is missing. Drinks were a bit of a challenge because there were so many ideas you can do with them, and we had a lot of drinks. I personally used my last shelf and some of my second to last shelf with a wire stand for drinks. Also make sure the labels of the bottles are showing.

The last thing to know is the laundry side. I used two baskets for extra detergent and some linens. I got them from Big Lots too. The laundry side was way easier because there weren’t so many items on the shelf.

In the end, you can do whatever is best for your pantry/laundry room. I’m just here to give you ideas of what you can do and know how to use it.

Items Used:

  • White Thin Bins
  • Basket for Linens
  • Basket for Extra Detergent
  • Grey or White Large Bins
  • 3-pack Stackable Baskets
  • Large Stackable Baskets
  • Broom and Mop Holder
  • Food Storage Containers
  • Crate for Lunchboxes
  • Wire Stands
  • Labels for Bins
  • Sign for Pantry
  • Signs for "Wash" and "Dry"
